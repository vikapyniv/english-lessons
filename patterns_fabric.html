Lesson objectives

1Recognise and accurately name common clothing patterns.
2Identify materials and connect them with qualities and suitable clothing.
3Use pattern + material + clothing combinations in extended speaking.

Warm-up

Activate familiar clothing vocabulary and prepare students to justify personal preferences.

Patterns

Explore the vocabulary visually, discuss personal preferences, and use the words in short speaking and writing tasks.

2

Explore the pattern cards

Stage 1 — Look and identify. Look at all the pictures and name any patterns you already know. Do not flip the cards yet.

Stage 2 — Discuss every card. Even when you do not know the English word, talk about the pattern: Do you like it? Would you wear it? Do you have anything like this in your wardrobe? Where do you usually see this pattern?

Stage 3 — Check and repeat. Click each card to reveal the word and definition. Say the word aloud and compare it with your original idea.

Complete the sentences

Combine one word from Box A with one word from Box B to make a natural phrase. Write the phrase in each gap and add more details.

Example

I think a wool jumper and corduroy trousers are perfect for cold weather.

If you spend time outside, they will keep you warm and protect you from the cold wind.
