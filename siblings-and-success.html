Task 5

“I’m the oldest child. I make the rules”, read a mug my family owned when I was small. “I’m the middle child. I am the reason there are rules.” And then, underneath: “I am the youngest child. I’m above the rules.”

We have lots of preconceptions about what the birth order of siblings means; that mug seemed perfectly accurate to me. The responsible, ambitious eldest child; the middle child who is difficult to deal with; and the easy-going youngest.

But what does this birth order mean when siblings grow up? Yes, people think that elder siblings are more ambitious, but is that really so and does it mean that they earn more?

We know that a person’s parents, spouse and children all have significant effects on how wealthy they may become. But the impact of siblings is not so easy to see.

Article
True or false

Siblings can push each other to achieve. Aditya Anand Kumar decided to move to the UK from India four years ago to go to university, partly because his eldest sister Dhivya had done it, too. She started a master’s in economics in 2021 at the University of Glasgow and later earned a PhD in Game Theory.

“The fact that my sister was already in the UK made me realize that it was possible to go. Once someone in your family is already there, it is not a dream anymore,” he says. “Looking at Dhivya, I get inspiration from her.”

Aditya, 24, and Dhivya, 31, now both work in London and live together. He’s a financial reporter, and she works for Amazon, and they earn about the same amount. Dhivya is slightly ahead in earnings if company shares are included.

Dhivya says that being the eldest meant she spoke more to their parents about their finances – and that this encouraged her to earn well. Now, the siblings support each other with career advice.

Aditya says he was lazier at school than Dhivya, but with time his results got even better than hers. Siblings pushing each other in this way is called the ‘sibling spillover effect’. Emma Zang, an academic at Yale University, says: “We found that having an academically successful older sibling positively affects younger siblings’ academic performance. He or she could be a very good role model.”
