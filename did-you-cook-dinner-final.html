Speaking practice

Build a real story by asking simple follow-up questions.

4

Tell me more!

Choose a category and start with only one short sentence. Your partner asks questions to discover the full story.

How to do it
  1. Choose one category.
  2. Student A says only one short sentence.
  3. Student B asks follow-up questions.
  4. Continue until the whole story is clear.
  5. Change roles and choose a new category.

Example

Category: A place

Student A: “It was very hot, but I loved it.”

Student B: Where were you? When did you go? Who were you with? What did you do there? Why did you love it?

Useful follow-up questions

When did it happen?Where were you?Who were you with?What did you do?What happened next?Why did you do it?How did you feel?Was it difficult?What was surprising?Would you do it again?
Keep talking for about 30 minutes. Try to discuss at least 6–8 categories.
